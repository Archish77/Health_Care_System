Understanding the Parameters

This guide explains the clinical metrics used by our algorithm to evaluate cardiovascular health.

Symptoms & Physical Indicators

Chest Pain Type Clinical category of chest discomfort.

Angina is chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart. Typical Angina follows a pattern (effort-based), while Atypical does not. Non-anginal pain is chest pain not related to the heart, and Asymptomatic means no pain is felt despite underlying issues.

Exercise Induced Angina Pain triggered by physical activity.

This indicates if a person experiences chest pain specifically when the heart is working harder (like walking uphill,gyming,running,walking,etc). It is a key indicator of coronary artery narrowing.

Laboratory Results

Cholestoral Amount of cholesterol in the blood.

Cholesterol is a waxy substance found in your blood. High levels can combine with other substances to form plaque, which sticks to the walls of your arteries (atherosclerosis).

Electrical & Stress Tests (ECG)

Resting ECG Results Electrical activity of the heart at rest.

Measures the rhythm and electrical signals. ST-T wave abnormalities can suggest lack of oxygen to the heart, while LVH suggests the heart muscle has thickened from overwork.

ST Depression Stress-induced heart strain.

This refers to a specific dip in the ECG line during exercise compared to rest. A larger depression usually indicates a higher risk of coronary heart disease.

Slope of ST Segment The "recovery" curve after peak exercise.

Describes the slope of the heart's recovery. An upsloping line is generally healthy, while a flat or downsloping line can be a sign of restricted blood flow.

Specialized Imaging

Major Vessels Fluoroscopy results (0-3)

Refers to the number of major coronary arteries that are clear and visible. A lower number of visible vessels often indicates narrowing or blockage.

Thalassemia Blood flow defect status

Indicates if blood flow to the heart is normal, has a permanent "fixed" defect, or a "reversible" defect that only appears under stress.
